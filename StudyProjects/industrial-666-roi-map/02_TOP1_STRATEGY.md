# Top 1% Strategy

## Top 1% Definition

In this project, "Top 1%" means top-tier personal industrial cognition and opportunity judgment, not factory-operator mastery of all production details.

| Dimension | Top 1% behavior |
|---|---|
| Boundary judgment | Can quickly tell what a small class includes, excludes, and how it differs from adjacent classes. |
| Mechanism judgment | Can explain how the industry creates value through materials, equipment, process, quality, customers, standards, and cost. |
| Opportunity judgment | Can rank career, startup, investment, research, content, and consulting opportunities by evidence and ROI. |
| Risk judgment | Can identify heavy-asset traps, policy traps, concept hype, low transparency, price wars, and cyclicality. |
| Transfer judgment | Can reuse one industry's logic to understand another industry's bottlenecks and opportunities. |
| Output ability | Can produce a credible industry memo, opportunity screen, or decision brief quickly. |

## 20% High-Leverage Knowledge

| Concept | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Industrial small class | Minimum unit for precise industry learning and ranking. |
| Value chain | Shows where profit, power, and bottleneck sit. |
| Process flow | Reveals quality, cost, yield, equipment, and safety constraints. |
| Core equipment | Often controls barrier, capex, maintenance, and import substitution. |
| Key materials | Controls performance, cost, supply security, and technology ceiling. |
| Quality indicators | Convert vague "advanced manufacturing" into measurable performance. |
| Customer certification | Explains why adoption is slow even when technology works. |
| Capex intensity | Separates personal opportunity from big-company opportunity. |
| Regulation and safety | Determines what can be done by individuals, startups, or large incumbents. |
| Industrial data availability | Determines whether AI, research, or investment tracking can compound. |
| Price cycle | Prevents confusing cyclical rebound with structural improvement. |
| Import substitution | Useful only when customer, certification, reliability, and cost evidence exist. |
| Industrial software layer | Shows where AI and software can amplify old industries. |
| Hidden champion logic | Low-profile components can have high margins and high stickiness. |
| Standard and certification | Creates barriers and trust in industrial markets. |
| Energy and emissions constraint | Important for costs, policy, technology routes, and long-term competitiveness. |
| Maintenance and aftermarket | Often creates more stable margin than first equipment sale. |
| Regional cluster | Reveals supply-chain density, labor, logistics, and policy advantages. |
| Personal entry path | Converts knowledge into career, startup, investment, content, or consulting action. |
| Evidence chain | Prevents fake certainty and hallucinated opportunity. |

## 80% Defer List

| Defer | Reason |
|---|---|
| Memorizing all 666 classes in code order | Low ROI unless tied to ranking, cluster, or decision use. |
| Deep process engineering for every class | Too expensive for first 90 days; only top classes deserve deep depth. |
| Market-size numbers without source | Creates false confidence. |
| Pure policy summary | Policy support is not the same as commercial viability. |
| Pure stock-theme tracking | Capital-market heat can distort industry understanding. |
| Factory-level implementation details before mechanism | Detail without framework is low retention. |
| Overbuilding Notion dashboards | Notion is a notebook, GitHub is source of truth. |

## Key Anti-Patterns

| Mistake | Correction |
|---|---|
| Thinking "industry is big" means "personal opportunity is big" | Personal ROI depends on accessible entry points, data, tools, and leverage. |
| Thinking "policy supports it" means "company will profit" | Need customer budget, technical path, margins, and adoption evidence. |
| Thinking "technology advanced" means "easy to monetize" | Advanced technology can be long-cycle, capital-heavy, or certification-heavy. |
| Thinking "traditional industry" means "low value" | Many low-profile components and services have strong margins and high switching cost. |
| Thinking "ranking is objective truth" | Ranking is a decision tool based on explicit assumptions, weights, and evidence. |

## Default ROI Lens

For every small class, ask:

1. What real industrial problem does it solve?
2. Who pays for it, and why now?
3. Where is the bottleneck: material, equipment, process, certification, customer, regulation, or capital?
4. What part is accessible to me through AI, research, content, consulting, product design, career, or investment?
5. What evidence would prove the opportunity is real?
